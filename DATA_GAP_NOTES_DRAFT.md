## Proposed Text for Methodology Page

### Data notes & limitations

**Source alignment:** Figures are drawn from Vanuatu Judiciary Annual Reports (PDFs) for 2017–2025. Where PDF values differed from earlier estimates, we have aligned the dashboard to the PDFs.

**Years covered:** 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025. 2017 and 2019 were previously missing and are now included.

**Domestic violence / protection orders:** DV filings and disposals are from the PDFs (e.g. 2025: 1,167 filings, 1,135 disposals; 2024: 1,226 / 1,113). Earlier years may have been estimated before verification.

**Charge orders:** The “charge orders” metric in this dashboard may not match “criminal registered” in the PDFs (e.g. Supreme Court 255 in 2025 vs dashboard series). If you notice mismatches, the definition in the source reports may differ and is under review.

**Gender breakdown:** The 2025 report states that gender breakdown will be published on the website. The dashboard uses prior-year patterns (about 63% male) for 2025. A note will be added once official 2025 figures are available.

**Island Court:** Island Court has no outcome data (Guilty, Not Guilty, Dismissed, etc.), attendance rates, productivity, or reserved judgments in the PDFs. These appear as “—” in the dashboard.

**Earlier reports (2017–2018, 2020):** PDF values applied. 2017 and 2018 Supreme Court filings were rescanned from PDFs (SC 2017→689, 2018→712). 2020 uses the corrected Annual Report: SC 866 filings, 1,181 disposals, 136% clearance, 806 pending; MC 2,231/2,278, 102% clearance; IC 383/372.

**2025 context:** Port Vila Magistrates Court had reduced capacity in early 2025 due to the December 2024 earthquake. This may affect 2025 Magistrates figures.

**Timeliness & productivity:** 2025 values (e.g. SC Criminal 183 days, MC 197 days; SC productivity 109, MC 269) reflect the latest PDF updates.
